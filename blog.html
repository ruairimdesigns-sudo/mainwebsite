003 - Open Collab #5

Connecting designers together


As part of an Open Collab session I was able to create these outcomes (among an unlimited number of combinations) with artists over the UK. For those who do not know Open Collab gives a set brief around the topic of a single word - this sessions being unity - and a grid to design on. From there you create your design of imagery or text and through their system they create randomised posters combining your art with another designers.

This was a great project to take part in as I was able to collaborate with artists over the UK/world and make contact with people I never would have otherwise. The outcomes alone were quite impressive as they merged the various styles of design on show and created that element of random experimentation, which often leads to the some of the best results in design. This all felt especially heart warming, if not quite poignant at the time of the covid.19 pandemic. Connecting designers and people is necessary always, but felt more so at this time of heightened isolation and I was grateful to take part in it.

002 - Mentors, Mentors, Mentors

My thoughts on mentors and my experience with them


So far in my short journey into the design industry I have come to feel as though it is an important aspect of to have these mentoring figures. It is hard to say exactly what describes a mentor - someone who you learn from, as inspired by, who guides you. If I think back, there are a few designers who have said specific phrases down the line that have stuck with me.

One of the earliest I can remember was being a very small child and an artist, a friend of my grandfather's once sat me down and gave me some tips. I recall him crumpling up a piece of paper after drawing on it and telling me to never be afraid to make mistakes and begin again - which is undoubtedly a necessary part to any designers process. A front end web designer, when I was about 16, told me to focus on learning keyboard short-cuts. Not something extremely profound, but it has been a sound piece of advice that has sped up my process. A designer who I admired, Anne, told me that she tries to mix with as large variety of people as possible, that all her friends are different. I cannot help but feel this has affected myself as a designer - looking to be inspired in as many places as possible and not limiting yourself to a single style, process or outcome can only improve your approach to design.

All in all, I am glad for the other artists and designers who I have been influenced by and have crossed paths with, in fact, it was a sort of mentor figure who encouraged me to create this blog.

001 - The Beginning

Starting a blog and what I hope to achieve with it


Hello, this is the beginning of my blog. Whoever you are reading this and for whatever purpose you have landed on my site, thank you. The purpose of this blog is to be able to talk through my processes, my thoughts about design and anything that I feel is relevant to the work that I make. I think it is vital to have some sort of voice in this industry and I suppose this is just another way for me to express mine.

Sometimes I think it is best to write down ideas, note thoughts and talk through patterns of thinking - which is what I plan on doing here. I also want to be able to give others a better insight into myself and to open up the possibility of discussion with like minded and unlike minded people.
